# MTDPrep — Monetisation Playbook

## Current pricing

- Free: 1 statement (soft gate via localStorage)
- Standard: £19/month — unlimited statements

## Validated positioning

MTD compliance for UK sole traders and landlords who use Excel and resist full accounting software. Closest competitor (Dext Solo) is B2B (sold to accountants, minimum 5-client bundle). MTDPrep is the only consumer-facing product in this gap.

## Growth levers

1. **SEO**: MTD-broad cluster (12,000–15,000 UK searches/mo). Publish 2 posts/week targeting long-tail MTD keywords. The existing 7-post blog gives a head start on this cluster.
2. **Programmatic pages**: one page per UK bank ("Lloyds Bank statement to Excel", "Barclays bank statement converter"). 20+ pages × 50–100 searches each — now supported by the product itself, since the app already works with any UK bank.
3. **123 Sheets referral**: 123 Sheets tells PDF users to "find a free PDF converter". A referral partnership or even a mention in their docs would drive direct, high-intent traffic.
4. **AccountingWEB**: an active community of UK accountants and sole traders. Content marketing or a paid directory listing could reach a qualified audience directly.

## Regulatory tailwind

- April 2026: ~780,000 people mandated (>£50k income)
- April 2027: +970,000 people (>£30k income)
- April 2028: +500,000 people (>£20k income)
- Total addressable market: ~2.25M by 2028. The market grows automatically as MTD thresholds phase in — no marketing spend required to expand the pool of people who legally need this product.

## Unit economics

- £19/month subscription
- ~£0.01–£0.03 per statement in Claude API costs (using `claude-haiku-4-5`)
- Gross margin: >99% at current prices — no server costs beyond Vercel's free/hobby tier, no storage costs (fully stateless)
- Break-even: a single paying customer covers the Vercel hobby plan (free) many times over
